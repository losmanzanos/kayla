Therapy for burnout, postpartum anxiety, eldest daughter syndrome & identity loss in Mesa, AZ.

You're Not Failing

You've been strong for everyone else.
It's your turn.

If you're exhausted in a way sleep doesn't fix — if you've been everything to everyone for so long you're not sure who you are outside of your roles — you're in the right place.

This isn't weakness. This is what happens when someone gives and gives without ever receiving. When caregiving becomes your entire identity. When the idea of your own needs feels selfish — or even dangerous.

Therapy for mothers at Mariposa isn't about making you a better mother. It's about helping you remember that you're a whole person who deserves to be held too.

What We Can Work On

This space was built for you

Maternal Burnout

The bone-deep exhaustion of constant caregiving that no amount of rest seems to touch. We work to understand what's depleting you — and rebuild in a way that's actually sustainable.

Eldest Daughter Syndrome

If you grew up as the responsible one, the caretaker, the emotional anchor of your family — those patterns didn't go away when you grew up. We can trace them, understand them, and create something different.

Postpartum Anxiety & Depression

Becoming a mother changes everything, including your brain. Postpartum anxiety and depression are medical conditions that respond to treatment. You don't have to white-knuckle through this.

Identity Loss in Caregiving

When motherhood (or caregiving) consumes your entire identity, it can feel like you've lost yourself. Therapy helps you reconnect with who you are beyond your roles.

Generational Patterns

The way you parent was partly shaped by how you were parented — which was shaped by how they were parented. Identifying and interrupting these cycles is some of the most meaningful work we do.

People-Pleasing & Fawning

The chronic tendency to prioritize everyone else's comfort at the expense of your own. We'll work with the parts of you that learned this was the safest way to be — and help them find new strategies.

The Approach

How we work with
maternal burnout & trauma

Most of the mothers I work with came to therapy reluctantly. They scheduled the appointment between school drop-off and a work deadline. They felt guilty taking an hour for themselves. Some cried in the parking lot before the first session.

That's exactly where we start. Not with a treatment plan, but with being seen.

IFS for Mothers

Internal Family Systems helps us get to know the parts of you that have been working overtime — the inner critic that says you're not doing enough, the protector who keeps everyone at arm's length, the exhausted part who just wants to disappear for a weekend. When those parts feel understood rather than managed, they naturally start to relax.

Healing the Eldest Daughter

Eldest daughter syndrome is a pattern, not a diagnosis — but its impact is real. It often looks like chronic anxiety, difficulty receiving help, extreme responsibility, resentment that feels shameful, and a deep sense that your needs are a burden. These patterns formed for a reason. In therapy, we work to honor that reason while freeing you from it.

Postpartum Support

Postpartum anxiety and depression are underdiagnosed and undertreated. If you're experiencing intrusive thoughts, rage, numbness, or a disconnect from your baby or yourself — that's not a reflection of who you are as a mother. It's a physiological and psychological response to an enormous transition. You deserve real support, not just reassurance that it will pass.

Good fit for you if:

  • You identify as an eldest or parentified child
  • You're experiencing maternal burnout
  • You're struggling postpartum
  • You can't stop over-functioning
  • You feel guilty for having needs
  • You've lost your sense of self in caregiving
  • You want to break generational cycles
  • You're ready to prioritize yourself

Questions

Things mothers often ask

I feel guilty spending money and time on therapy. Is this worth it?

The guilt you feel is itself a symptom of what we'd work on together. The research is clear: a mother's mental health directly impacts her children's development and her family's wellbeing. Investing in your healing is one of the most generous things you can do for the people you love — and it's long overdue to do it for yourself.

What is eldest daughter syndrome exactly?

It's a term for a pattern common in eldest (or parentified) daughters — people who were given adult responsibilities in childhood, became the family caretaker or emotional regulator, and carried that role into adulthood. It often shows up as extreme responsibility, difficulty setting limits, chronic anxiety, resentment, and deep difficulty receiving care. It's not a clinical diagnosis, but the impact is real and it responds well to IFS work.

Do you work with postpartum anxiety and depression?

Yes. Postpartum anxiety and depression are two of the most common perinatal mental health conditions, and both are highly treatable. If you're experiencing anxiety, intrusive thoughts, rage, numbness, or disconnection — that's not a character flaw, it's a medical and psychological response to an enormous life transition. Please reach out. Early support makes a real difference.

Can I do virtual sessions?

Yes. I offer secure virtual sessions throughout Arizona. For many mothers, the flexibility of telehealth makes consistent therapy actually possible. You can attend from home during nap time, your parked car, your lunch break — wherever you have privacy and 50 minutes.
