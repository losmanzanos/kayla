Your Therapist

Hi, I'm Kayla —
and I'm glad you're here

I became a therapist because I believe every person carries wisdom they haven't fully met yet. My job isn't to fix you — it's to help you find your own way back to yourself.

I specialize in working with women navigating anxiety, perfectionism, trauma, and the kind of exhaustion that comes from spending too long taking care of everyone else. Whether we're processing the past, strengthening coping skills, or rebuilding self-trust — my goal is to create a space where you feel understood and genuinely empowered.

"I primarily use IFS because I've found that most symptoms can be addressed by getting to know the part of you that's showing up — then giving that part new skills to help you cope with challenging emotions and situations."

I offer both in-person sessions in Mesa, AZ and virtual therapy throughout Arizona, with flexible weekday and evening availability to fit your life.

My Philosophy

Therapy isn't about
being fixed

I believe every symptom — the anxiety, the perfectionism, the fawning, the shutting down — was once a brilliant adaptation. It was your system doing its best to protect you when the world felt unsafe or unpredictable.

My work isn't to eliminate those parts of you. It's to help them feel safe enough to relax. To help you access the parts of yourself that are wise, grounded, and genuinely capable of healing — because they were always there.

IFS (Internal Family Systems) is the heart of how I work. But I draw from EMDR, somatic therapy, DBT, and attachment-based approaches depending on what each person needs. I'll never fit you into a protocol. I'll always start with you.

What I Value

The principles behind my practice

01

Curiosity Over Judgment

Every part of you deserves curiosity — not shame. I approach whatever you bring with genuine interest, not a predetermined agenda for how you "should" be.

02

Your Pace, Always

Healing isn't linear and it can't be rushed. We go at the speed your nervous system can handle, which means some sessions feel slow — and that's exactly right.

03

The Whole Person

Mind, body, relationships, history — all of it matters. I'm not just treating a diagnosis. I'm sitting with a human being whose experience is complex and worthy of depth.

04

Real Relationships

The therapeutic relationship is itself healing. I show up as a real person — warm, honest, sometimes funny — because authentic connection is part of the work.

05

Earned Expertise

Six years of clinical practice, ongoing training in IFS and EMDR, and a commitment to staying current. Evidence-based, but never cold or clinical.

06

You Already Have It

I don't believe I give you insight. I believe you already carry it — and my job is to create conditions where you can access what's always been yours.

Common Questions

A few things people ask first

Do you take insurance?

Mariposa Mental Wellness is self-pay only at $165/session. Kayla provides superbills for out-of-network reimbursement — many PPO plans cover 40–80% after your deductible. HSA/FSA cards accepted. More on fees →

Do you offer virtual sessions?

Yes — secure telehealth for anyone in Arizona, plus in-person sessions at the Mesa office (1845 S Dobson Rd, Suite 101). Most clients choose whichever fits their schedule week to week.

What if I've had therapy before and it didn't help?

That's actually one of the most common things Kayla hears. Approach and fit matter enormously. IFS and trauma-focused work feel very different from general talk therapy — many clients find this is the first time they experience real, lasting change.

The easiest step is the free 15-minute consultation — no paperwork, no commitment. Reach out here or call/text (480) 605-0846.
